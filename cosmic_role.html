Dark matter is more than just an astronomical curiosity; it plays a fundamental role in shaping our universe.

Galaxy Formation

The gravitational pull of dark matter is believed to be instrumental in the formation of galaxies. In the early universe, dark matter clumps served as seeds around which gas and dust coalesced to form galaxies.

Cosmic Web

Dark matter forms an invisible scaffold known as the 'cosmic web', a vast network of filaments that stretch across the universe, guiding the distribution and movement of galaxies and galaxy clusters.

Influence on Cosmic Evolution

Dark matter's gravitational influence affects the rate of expansion of the universe and plays a crucial role in cosmological evolution. Understanding dark matter is key to piecing together the puzzle of our universe's history and future.
